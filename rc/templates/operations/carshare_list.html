Car sharing programs enable faculty, staff, and students to rent vehicles for short periods of time. Gas, insurance, and roadside assistance are all taken care of by the car sharing company. Such programs reduce the need for people to bring their own vehicle to campus, thereby freeing up parking spaces, and provide a cost-effective alternative to car ownership. Universities often subsidize participation in car sharing programs as part of a comprehensive transportation demand management strategy. Traditionally, for insurance purposes, car sharing has been limited to people aged 21 and older, thereby limiting many college students from participating. Several of car sharing companies now offer programs that enable students aged 18 and older to participate.

This resource lists existing car sharing programs on campus. Programs are categorized according to the car sharing partner used by each campus.
